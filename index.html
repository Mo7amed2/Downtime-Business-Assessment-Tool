Financial Perspective

Total Downtime Cost
—
What it means: The total downtime financial loss for the selected analysis period.
Formula: Total Downtime Cost (input)
Why it matters: Provides a clear baseline for improvement decisions.
Cost per Downtime Hour
—
What it means: Estimated hourly financial exposure when downtime occurs.
Formula: Total Downtime Cost ÷ Downtime Hours
Why it matters: Quantifies the value of eliminating one downtime hour.
Annual Financial Exposure
—
What it means: Estimated annual cost if the same trend continues.
Formula: (Total Downtime Cost ÷ Period Days) × 365
Why it matters: Supports budgeting, ROI, and investment justification.
Potential Annual Savings
—
What it means: Estimated annual savings based on your improvement target.
Formula: Annual Financial Exposure × (Reduction Target ÷ 100)
Why it matters: Connects reliability actions with measurable business value.

Operations Perspective

Total Downtime Hours
—
What it means: Total downtime hours in the analysis period.
Formula: Downtime Hours (input)
Why it matters: Direct driver of lost capacity and service disruption.
Availability %
—
What it means: Percentage of time the equipment was available for operation.
Formula: ((Operating Hours − Downtime Hours) ÷ Operating Hours) × 100
Why it matters: A core reliability KPI with direct operational impact.
Downtime per Failure (Average)
—
What it means: Average downtime duration per failure event.
Formula: Downtime Hours ÷ Number of Failures
Why it matters: Distinguishes quick fixes from major breakdowns.

Maintenance Perspective

MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)
—
What it means: Average operating time between failures (reliability indicator).
Formula: Operating Hours ÷ Number of Failures
Why it matters: Higher MTBF indicates fewer failures and stronger reliability.
MTTR (Mean Time To Repair)
—
What it means: Average time to restore equipment after a failure.
Formula: Total Repair Hours ÷ Number of Failures
Why it matters: Lower MTTR reduces disruption and cost exposure.
Failure Rate
—
What it means: Failures per 1,000 operating hours.
Formula: (Number of Failures ÷ Operating Hours) × 1000
Why it matters: Enables comparison across assets and sites.
